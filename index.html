The measurement framework (same in every state)

Every custom program uses the same method, so the measurement answers do not change by state. Only the dollars and timing do.

The savings equation

Annual kWh saved = ( kW before − kW after ) × yearly run hours

Log the power before and after on the equipment, multiply the drop by run hours. Whole-building work compares the utility meter before and after instead.

What to measure with, and how long

A calibrated power logger, cellular or standalone is your choice. Duration depends on how much the load varies: a few days to two weeks for steady loads, two to four weeks for cyclical loads, up to a full year for whole-building or California's measured path. Log until the data is solid to 90 percent confidence and 10 percent precision.

How incentives are set

Either a rate per kWh saved, or a percentage of project cost up to a cap. A program uses one, not both. Getting pre-approved before you start work is required on every custom path.

Target customer playbook (who to pursue, ranked)

The same scoring logic, applied to customer types. Conversation ease is how reachable and motivated the decision-maker is. Tech fit is how well our stack (HVAC optimizer, solar thermal, CryogenX4, harmonizers) produces measurable savings there. Multiplied, they set the order. The kWh band tells you whether a site clears thresholds like Illinois's 500,000 kWh upfront rule.

Note: CryogenX4 risks being classed as maintenance in some programs, so on refrigeration targets it should ride inside a larger measured retrofit, not lead. Harmonizers rarely clear kWh rebates and belong in demand-charge conversations.
